Project Overview

Saint Laurent de Gaseke is proud to showcase a diverse portfolio of student-led projects and institutional initiatives. These projects demonstrate the practical application of theoretical knowledge across all our trade programs. Each project represents months of dedication, collaboration, and innovation from our talented students and faculty.

We believe in learning by doing. Our students work on real-world projects that solve actual problems, develop marketable skills, and create lasting value for our community.

Software Development (SOD) Projects

01

School Management System

Full-Stack Web Application

A comprehensive web-based platform for managing student records, attendance, grades, and communication between students, teachers, and parents. Built using modern web technologies including HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and a backend database system. Features include real-time notifications, secure authentication, and intuitive dashboards.

  • HTML5 & CSS3
  • JavaScript
  • Database Management
02

E-Commerce Platform

Online Shopping Solution

An interactive online marketplace enabling local artisans and small businesses to sell their products globally. Features include product catalog management, shopping cart functionality, secure payment integration, order tracking, and customer feedback systems. Responsive design ensures seamless experience across all devices.

  • Frontend Framework
  • Payment Gateway
  • Database Design
03

Mobile Learning App

Educational Technology

A mobile application designed to provide students with on-demand access to educational resources, tutorials, and practice materials for their respective trade programs. Includes interactive quizzes, video lessons, progress tracking, and peer collaboration features. Available on both iOS and Android platforms.

  • Mobile Development
  • API Integration
  • Cloud Storage

Electrical Technology (ETE) Projects

04

Solar Power Installation

Renewable Energy Implementation

Students designed and installed a complete solar power system on the school campus, providing clean energy for classroom operations. The project included panel installation, inverter configuration, wiring systems, and maintenance protocols. This practical experience demonstrates real-world applications of electrical theory and sustainable energy solutions.

  • Solar Panels
  • Inverter Systems
  • Electrical Wiring
05

Building Electrical System

Infrastructure Installation

Complete design and installation of electrical systems in new school buildings, including distribution panels, circuit breakers, lighting systems, and power outlets. Students learned about electrical safety codes, load calculations, and proper installation techniques. The project ensures all systems meet international safety standards and government regulations.

  • Circuit Design
  • Safety Compliance
  • Installation Techniques
06

Backup Generator Setup

Power Management Solution

Installation and configuration of a backup diesel generator system with automatic transfer switches. Students gained hands-on experience with generator operation, maintenance, fuel management, and safety protocols. The system ensures continuous power supply during electricity outages, critical for laboratory and server operations.

  • Generator Configuration
  • Transfer Switches
  • Maintenance Procedures

Building Construction (BDC) Projects

07

New Classroom Block

Infrastructure Development

Students actively participated in the construction of new classroom facilities, from foundation work through to finishing touches. The project involved site preparation, concrete pouring, masonry work, and structural assembly. Students learned about building codes, material selection, quality control, and project management through hands-on experience on an actual construction site.

  • Foundations
  • Masonry Work
  • Finishing
08

Laboratory Building Renovation

Building Refurbishment

Complete renovation of the existing laboratory building to meet modern safety and functionality standards. Work included structural reinforcement, rewiring, plumbing updates, and interior redesign. Students developed skills in structural assessment, renovation planning, and coordinating multiple trades. The renovated facility now provides state-of-the-art learning spaces.

  • Structural Assessment
  • Renovation Planning
  • Multi-Trade Coordination
09

Dormitory Complex Construction

Residential Infrastructure

Comprehensive construction project for student accommodation facilities designed to house 200+ students. The project encompassed site management, structural work, plumbing and drainage systems, electrical integration, and interior finishing. Students gained experience in large-scale project management and complex coordination between different construction phases and trades.

  • Project Management
  • Large-Scale Construction
  • Systems Integration

Electronics & Telecommunication (ELT) Projects

10

Campus Network Infrastructure

Telecommunications System

Design and implementation of a campus-wide networking infrastructure providing high-speed internet connectivity to all buildings. Students installed fiber optic cables, configured switches, routers, and access points. The project included network security implementation, bandwidth management, and redundancy systems to ensure uninterrupted connectivity across campus.

  • Fiber Optics
  • Network Configuration
  • Security Systems
11

Smart Building Automation

IoT and Automation

Development of an intelligent building management system controlling lighting, temperature, and security across school facilities. Students programmed microcontrollers, installed sensors, and created automated workflows for energy efficiency. The system reduces utility costs while providing real-time monitoring and remote control capabilities accessible from mobile devices.

  • Microcontrollers
  • Sensor Networks
  • IoT Programming
12

Emergency Communication System

Safety and Security

Implementation of an integrated emergency communication system including public address systems, intercom networks, and mobile alerts. Students configured audio distribution, tested communication clarity, and integrated the system with security protocols. This ensures rapid communication capability during emergencies and important announcements across the entire campus.

  • Audio Systems
  • Intercom Networks
  • Alert Integration
